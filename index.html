Pitzik4's Ludum Dare games

Ludum Dare is a thrice-yearly jam during which people from around the world make games in either 48 or 72 hours, at their option. These games are then judged by fellow entrants in a number of categories. Your prize is your product, but many consider the ratings they receive a valuable form of feedback on their growth and their current strengths and weaknesses. I've participated 13 times under the name of Pitzik4.

Here are the games I made. The versions available here are HTML/JS ports of the originals; I originally used Java for LD23--LD25, then Flash for LD26--LD31 (except for LD30, which was, uh, a weird one). I used HTML/JS from LD32 onwards, but my LD34 game had an incredibly sketchy graphics engine built on HTML and CSS, so I ported it to a faster and more compatible canvas-based engine.

Note: I've tried to make these ports as faithful to the originals as possible, bug-for-bug. To make this possible, and to significantly lessen the difficulty of the porting process, I transpiled the original code rather than writing the new JavaScript myself. Each game required a lot of rejiggering to run, and some required a chainsaw to run fast because I didn't know what optimization was when I was 13. There are a few extremely minor discrepancies, but I don't see them as anything important. If you find a bug, just assume it was also present in the original. I haven't ported every game, but all of the least worst ones are here.

The games

  1. Earth Evacuation (LD23, 48h): An incredibly confusing resource management game with no audio.
  2. Untitled (LD24, 72h): A survival platformer. The easiest way to win is by not doing anything.
  3. Prophecy of the Villain (LD25, 72h): An unfinished top-down RPG that does its best to lampoon stuff.
  4. D.RE.U (LD26, 48h): A self-loathing puzzle platformer with an interesting mechanic.
  5. Jackton Factory (LD27, 72h): A lightning-fast point-and-click adventure game with an even more interesting mechanic.
  6. Wiped (LD28, 48h): A severely half-baked story-centric platformer.
  7. Meddle (LD29, 48h): A short, kinda weird puzzle platformer with a mechanic so interesting it won 1st place in the innovation category! Hard to get the hang of, but I've linked to the refurbished version; the original was even harder.
  8. Guide 2 Where? (LD32, 72h): A story game with an interesting mechanic and a new art style.
  9. Monster Mind (LD33, 72h): A confusing endless ascension game with no point.
  10. I can. (LD34, 72h): A pensive, slow-paced story about growing up.
  11. Linear Motion (LD35, 72h): A minimalistic little sandbox where there's nothing to do but explore.
